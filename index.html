Auxora Nanolabs

Auxora Nanolabs is a modular microgravity simulator designed to study plant growth in space-like conditions. It replicates environmental factors such as light, airflow, and hydration control to help researchers understand how plants adapt and thrive beyond Earth.

What is Auxora Nanolabs about?

Auxora Nanolabs replicates plant growth experiments under simulated microgravity using an advanced control and sensing system. Through our custom software, users can monitor and adjust environmental variables — like LED light intensity, water delivery, and airflow direction — in real time. The system integrates a time-lapse camera and environmental sensors to collect continuous data, allowing researchers to analyze plant responses and optimize growth conditions for space missions. The Universal Nanolab will enable hands-on learning in classrooms of all grades throughout the nation due to its off-the-shelf design and seamless integration with the Nanolab application.

Key Features

  • Modular Design: Three interchangeable modules — Control, Experiment, and Sensor — allow for a variety of experimental setups.
  • Smart Integration: Pin header buses automatically recognize connected modules and adjust system parameters accordingly.
  • Automated Controls: Peristaltic pump feeds water precisely, while a continuous servo motor rotates to vary wind direction and strengthen roots.
  • Environmental Simulation: PC fan mount in the Sensor Module replicates atmospheric wind; LED array provides adjustable light spectrums.
  • Data Logging Software: Monitor and record plant growth metrics directly from your computer.
  • Aerospace-Grade Build: Constructed with durable PEI 9085 for use in extreme environments.
  • Advanced Electronics: Powered by an ESP-32 microcontroller with integrated VOC detection, camera feed, and automated control routines.
